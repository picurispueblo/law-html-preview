Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

10.02.050 Records Maintenance and Protection.

(a) A record of all hearings under this Code shall be made and preserved until the jurisdiction of the Court has ended. All Court and law enforcement records concerning a minor shall be kept separate from the records of adults.

(b) All records are confidential and shall not be open to inspection other than by Court personnel. The following persons are subject to regulations adopted by the Court:

(1) The minor and his/her attorney;

(2) The minor's parent(s), guardian(s), Indian custodian(s), or custodian(s);

(3) The Juvenile Probation Officer or child welfare caseworker;

(4) The prosecutor; and

(5) Any other person granted permission by Court Order.

(c) Each person who inspects a minor's record shall, in writing agree, to maintain the confidentiality of the record. Failure to abide by the agreement shall constitute Contempt of Court.

(d) All records of a minor shall be sealed when the minor is no longer subject to the Court's jurisdiction, and thereafter, released only by Court Order following notice to the parties, and, only if needed, for a Court proceeding concerning a serious criminal charge or similar circumstance of compelling need. This paragraph shall not be construed to permit the release of information for other purposes, such as background checks conducted for employment, licensing, or security clearances. Adoption records shall be released as allowed in Section 10- 13-9.
