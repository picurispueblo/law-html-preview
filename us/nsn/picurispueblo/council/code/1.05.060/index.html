Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

1.05.060 Disbarment and Discipline.

(a) Whenever it is shown to the Chief Judge of the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court that an attorney admitted to practice before the Courts of the Pueblo of Picuris has been disbarred or suspended from the practice of law by or before the highest court of any state, such attorney shall immediately be given notice that he/she shall be indefinitely suspended from further practice before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court unless he/she can appear within ten (10) days and show good cause why an order to the effect should not be made.

(b) Any Judge that finds an attorney admitted to practice before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court to be in contempt of Court may, in addition to any other sanction imposed, order the attorney to appear within ten (10) days and show cause why he/she should not be suspended from practicing before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court.

(c) The Chief Judge may, upon receiving a written, verified complaint, which indicates that an attorney admitted to practice before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court has acted in an unethical or improper manner while functioning as an attorney, order such attorney to appear and defend him/herself at a hearing to hear all evidence relevant to the matter, and may order the suspension of such an attorney if such appears reasonably necessary or appropriate.

(d) All suspensions from practicing before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court shall be for an indefinite period unless the Chief Judge specifically orders otherwise. An attorney suspended for an indefinite period, or one suspended for a specific time, may petition the Tribal Court for permission to reapply for admission to practice at the end of one (1) year or the specific period of suspension, and such permission shall be granted if it is made to appear, at a hearing or otherwise as the Court shall direct, that he/she has been adequately reproved and now appears willing to conduct him/herself in a proper manner, and that the petitioner has been reinstated to practice if previously disbarred or suspended in another jurisdiction

(e) Any person appearing as lay counsel for another may be suspended from further appearance as such for misconduct or improper behavior by any Judge upon the same conditions of notice and hearing provided professional attorneys.
