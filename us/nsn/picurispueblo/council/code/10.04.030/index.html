Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

10.04.030 Juvenile Probation Officer.

(a) The Court shall appoint from among the officers presently employed by the Eight Northern Pueblos Council Peacekeepers Program a Children's Court Juvenile Probation Officer who shall carry out the duties of this office.

(b) The Juvenile Probation Officer shall not give any testimony in any court proceeding which may lead to the filing of juvenile offender charges against a minor but may testify for purposes of revoking probation.

(c) The duties and powers of the Juvenile Probation Officer shall be to:

(1) Make preliminary inquiries and social studies, and such other investigations as the Judge may direct and to keep written records of such investigations or studies;

(2) Make reports as provided in this Code or as directed by the Judge;

(3) Conduct informal adjustment hearings;

(4) Place a minor in detention or shelter care as provided in this Code;

(5) Identify and develop resources on the Pueblo of Picuris to enhance each minor's potential as a valuable member of the tribal community;

(6) Explain to the minor, if old enough and to the parent(s), guardian(s), Indian custodian(s) or custodian(s), the meaning and conditions of probation or protective supervision and shall give necessary instructions;

(7) Keep informed concerning the conduct and conditions of each minor on probation or under protective supervision and shall report thereon to the Judge as directed;

(8) Use all suitable methods to aid minors on probation or under protective supervision to bring about improvements in their conduct or condition;

(9) Perform such other duties in connection with the care, custody or transportation of children as the Court may require; and

(10) Have the powers of law enforcement officers for purposes of this Code but shall, whenever possible, refrain from exercising such powers except in urgent situations in which a regular law enforcement officer is not immediately available.
