Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

10.06.060 Manner of Service of Court Orders.

(a) Who Serves Court Orders: Service of summons, subpoena or other Court Order shall be made by an officer of BIA Law Enforcement or the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Sheriff; but upon request of the Court such service may be made by the Tribal Sheriff or by another suitable person selected by the Court.

(b) Place of Service: Service of summons, subpoena or other Court Order on the Pueblo of Picuris may be made by delivering a copy thereof to the person summoned, subpoenaed or entitled to a copy of the Order; provided, however, that parents of a child living together in their home may both be served personally by delivering to either parent copies of the summons, one copy for each parent.

(c) Service by Mail: If the Court is satisfied that personal service of the summons or order is impractical under the circumstances, the Court may order service by registered mail, with a return receipt requested to be signed by the addressee only, to be addressed at the last known address or the person to be served on the Pueblo of Picuris. Service shall be complete upon return to the Court of the signed receipt.

(d) Other Service: If the parent or guardian of any minor required to be summoned by this Code cannot be found within the Pueblo of Picuris, the minor's presence within the Pueblo shall confer jurisdiction on the Court in proceedings in children's cases under this Code as to any absent parent(s), guardian(s), Indian custodian(s), or custodian(s) provided that due notice has been given in one of the following manners:

(1) If the address of the parent or guardian is unknown, by sending him/her a copy of the summons by registered mail with return receipt requested to be signed by the addressee only, or by personal service outside the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris. Service by registered mail shall be complete upon return to the Court of the signed receipt.

(2) If the address or whereabouts of the guardian outside of the Pueblo, cannot, after diligent inquiry, be ascertained, by publishing a summons in a newspaper having general circulation on the Pueblo. The summons shall be published once a week for four (4) successive weeks. Service shall be complete on the day of the last publication. Proof of such service shall be filed with the Court.
