Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

11.01.020 Purpose Construction and Severability.

(a) Findings: The Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Council hereby finds as follows:

(1) The increasing volume and variety of solid waste and hazardous waste being generated on the Pueblo of Picuris and the often-inadequate existing methods of managing solid waste and hazardous waste are creating conditions that threaten the public health, safety and welfare of tribal members and residents of the Pueblo of Picuris by contributing to land, air and water pollution, to the production of flies, rodents and litter, to the waste of dwindling natural resources, and to the general deterioration of the Pueblo of Picuris environment.

(2) The people of the Pueblo of Picuris have a primary interest in the protection and control of the land and other natural resources affected by the improper disposal of solid waste within the Pueblo of Picuris, and the quality of such land and other natural resources must be protected to insure the health, economic, aesthetic and cultural well-being of the Pueblo of Picuris People.

(3) Open dumping is particularly harmful to public health, contaminates surface and underground water resources, including drinking water from underground and surface supplies and pollutes the land, air and water of the Pueblo of Picuris.

(4) Inadequate and environmentally unsound practices for the disposal or use of solid waste and hazardous waste have created greater amounts of soil, air and water pollution and other problems for public health and the environment on the Pueblo of Picuris. The traditional methods of solid waste and hazardous waste management may not meet future requirements for eliminating environmental pollution and conserving natural resources.

(5) Pursuant to federal law as determined by the United States Supreme Court in such cases as Montana v. United States, 450 U.S. 544 (1981) and reaffirmed in the Strate v. A-1 Contractors 117 S. Ct. 1404 (1997), the Pueblo of Picuris possesses the inherent sovereign authority to regulate within its exterior boundaries solid waste disposal that affects fundamental tribal interests and public health and safety, including when such activities conducted by non-members of the Tribe on privately owned land within the exterior boundaries of Pueblo of Picuris.

(6) Under the Solid Waste Disposal Act, 42 U.S.C. ยง6903(13) (as part of the Resource Conservation and Recovery Act), "Indian Tribe" is included within the definition of "municipality" for purposes of the Act.

(7) Methods of solid waste management emphasizing source reduction, recovery and recycling of all solid waste are essential to the long-range preservation of the health, safety and welfare of the public, to the economic productivity of the Pueblo of Picuris, to the environmental quality of the Pueblo of Picuris and to the conservation of natural resources.

(8) Disposal of solid waste and hazardous waste in or on the land without careful planning and management will present a danger to the public health and environment.

(9) Methods are available to separate recoverable materials from solid waste. The recovery and conservation of such material can produce numerous benefits to the Pueblo of Picuris, including economic benefits in the way of savings realized by conserving material in order to reduce the value or quantity of materials that ultimately become solid waste.

(10) It is in the best interests of the Pueblo of Picuris and the residents of the Pueblo of Picuris to establish and maintain a comprehensive integrated tribal solid waste management plan, the objectives which will be to manage and control solid waste and to prohibit the introduction of hazardous waste into the Pueblo of Picuris in order to protect the health, safety and welfare of tribal members; to preserve the environment; and to provide for the maximum reuse of the resources contained in solid waste.

(11) Such protection of the Pueblo of Picuris resources is not adequately provided for under existing legislation, and such protection will be furthered by the passage of, adoption and implementation of this Code.

(b) Purpose: The general purposes of this Code are to:

(1) finance, implement, regulate, and enforce environmental standards and criteria, orders, and permit conditions, and exercise comprehensive Tribal regulatory authority over all solid waste and hazardous waste disposal matters within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris;

(2) protect fundamental Tribal cultural, ceremonial, religious, fishery, and economic stability of residential, agricultural, commercial, industrial, forest, wetlands, riparian and environmentally sensitive lands within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris;

(3) prevent the deterioration of the environment and increase the standard of living, quality of life, health, safety and welfare of all persons within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris;

(4) provide and promote Tribal environmental protection and services within the Pueblo of Picuris and to regulate environmental activities under principles of Tribal sovereignty; and

(5) prevent air, water and land from solid and hazardous waste pollution, including contamination of the Tribe's aquifers, ground waters, surface waters, drinking water supplies and other natural resources.

(c) Authority and Scope:

Authority: This Pueblo of Picuris Solid Waste Code is hereby adopted by The Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Council pursuant to Section 11 of the Pueblo of Picuris Law and Order Code and authorizing the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Council to undertake such actions.

Scope: The provisions of this Code shall apply to all existing and proposed solid waste disposal activities which have the potential to affect cultural, ceremonial, religious, fishery, seasonal residential, public health, safety, welfare, land, air or water quality and other fundamental interests of the Tribe, including such activities conducted by non-members of the Tribe or on privately owned lands. Activities to be regulated include but are not limited to:

(1) Landfills and open dumps;

(2) Storage of animal waste;

(3) Automobile graveyards and junkyards;

(4) Landfilling of sludge or septic system waste;

(5) Individual, residential, industrial, commercial or agricultural sewage treatment facilities; and

(6) All other activities that involve the storage, collection, transportation or disposal of solid and/or hazardous waste within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris;

(7) Any and all types of industrial or investigative derived wastes including soils, sediments, aqueous liquids, decontaminated fluids, construction and demolition wastes.

(d) Applicability: Because any violation of this Code or any regulation adopted thereunder will demonstrably and seriously impact the environment, including land, water and air, natural resources, public health, safety, welfare, political integrity and economic security of the Pueblo of Picuris, this Code, and any regulations adopted thereunder shall apply to: (1) all persons within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris, without exception, including but not limited to, all Tribal members, and all other persons on the Pueblo of Picuris, including any Indians who are member of other Indian tribes, all non-Indians, and any other person as defined under this Code; and (2) all places and lands located anywhere within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris including all trust and non-trust lands, and notwithstanding the issuance of any patent, fee, allotment, right-of-way, lease, or any real property interest of any kind, held by any person as defined in this Code.

(e) Consensual Relations Among Non-Members, The Tribe and Tribal Members: Any person who is not a member of the Tribe who uses land anywhere within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris, whether trust or non-trust land, enters into consensual relationships with the Tribe or its members, through commercial dealings, contracts, leases or other arrangements. Such person's use of land involving storage, collection, transportation and disposal of solid or hazardous waste on the Pueblo of Picuris will have a demonstrably serious impact upon the environment, natural resources, public health, safety, welfare, political integrity and economic security of the Tribe and its members, unless such use is in compliance with the provisions of this Code, and any regulations promulgated hereunder.
