Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

11.01.270 Enforcement Program/Administrative Procedures

Enforcement Policy: It is the policy of the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department to encourage informal, practical, result-oriented resolution of alleged violations and actions needed to prevent damage to Pueblo of Picuris resources or harm to the health, safety or welfare of the Pueblo of Picuris population. It is also the policy of the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department, consistent with the principles of due process, to provide effective procedures for enforcement.

Enforcement Agency: The Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department shall be responsible for enforcing the provisions of this Ordinance. Specifically, the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department shall conduct investigations when a complaint is received by the Bureau of Indian Affairs or other tribal agency believes that a violation of this Ordinance has occurred.

Enforcement Activities: Where a written and verified complaint shall be filed with the Bureau of Indian Affairs, New Mexico State Police for Taos County (per Special Law Enforcement Commissions in Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Council Resolution 2014-10) and the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department and reviewed by the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department alleging that, or where, the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department itself shall have cause to believe that, any person is violating any solid waste regulation or permit condition, the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department shall cause a prompt investigation to be made.

Notice of Violation: Cease and Desist Order: If the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department finds after an investigation pursuant to this Ordinance that a violation of any regulation or permit condition exists, the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department shall promptly notify both the alleged violator and the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Council, in writing.

In the case of an apparent violation of this Ordinance, the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department is authorized to issue a Notice of Violation to the person(s) apparently responsible for the violation, and, if the apparent violation occurred on property owned by a person other than the alleged violator, a Notice of Violation shall also be issued to the landowner.

In the case of a continuing violation or a threatened violation, the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department is authorized to issue a Cease and Desist Order to prevent the violation from continuing or occurring.

Failure to comply with a Cease and Desist Order shall constitute a violation of this Ordinance. Both a Notice of Violation and a Cease and Desist Order may be issued for a single incident. A Notice of Violation will include a Summons to appear before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court at an enforcement hearing at a specified time and date, and shall advise the alleged violator that failure to appear may result in the imposition of civil penalties.

If a Cease and Desist Order is issued without an accompanying Notice of Violation, the Order will inform the recipient that failure to comply with the Order will constitute a violation of this Ordinance which will result in the issuance of a Notice of Violation and may result in the imposition of civil penalties.

Informal Conferences: The Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department shall afford the landowner or his or her representative reasonable opportunities to discuss proposed enforcement actions at an informal conference prior to taking further enforcement action, unless the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department determines that there may be either imminent environmental damage to a Pueblo of Picuris resource or adverse impact upon the health, safety and welfare of the Pueblo of Picuris population. Informal conferences may be used at any stage in the enforcement proceedings, except that the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department may refuse to conduct informal conferences with respect to any matter pending before the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court.

Reports Required: The Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department shall keep written notes of the date and place of the conference, the persons in attendance, the subject matter discussed and any decisions reached with respect to further enforcement action.

Enforcement Hearings: If the landowner and the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department are unable to resolve the matter via an informal conference, the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court is authorized to conduct adjudicatory hearings to determine if a violation of this Ordinance has occurred.

In such a hearing the Pueblo of Picuris Solid Waste Department Manager, in cooperation with the Pueblo of Picuris Environment Department Director, in cooperation with the Tribal Sheriff shall present the case to the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court to establish that the person(s) charged has (have) committed a violation of this Ordinance. Any person so charged shall be entitled, at his or her own expense, to be represented by an attorney or other representative.

(a) Burden of Proof. The Director of the Pueblo of Picuris Solid Waste Department Manager shall have the burden of proving that a violation of this Ordinance has occurred and that a person charged was responsible for the violation. The Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court shall rule that a violation of this Ordinance has occurred if it finds that the charges are supported by substantial evidence and that a preponderance of the credible evidence supports a finding that a violation has occurred.

(b) Enforcement Orders. Within thirty (30) days after the date of any enforcement hearing, the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court shall issue a written decision. If the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court determines that a violation has occurred and that the person(s) charged was (were) responsible for the violation, the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court shall include an Enforcement Order.
