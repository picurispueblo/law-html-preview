Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

10.15.030 Enforcement of Attendance Law.

(a) The Board of Education of the local school district and the governing body of a public and/or charter school shall initiate the enforcement of the provisions of the Compulsory School Attendance Law [22-12-1 NMSA 1978] for students enrolled in their respective schools.

(b) To initiate enforcement of the provisions of the Compulsory School Attendance Law against an habitual truant, a local school board or governing authority of a private and/or charter school or its authorized representatives shall give written notice of the habitual truancy by certified mail to or by personal service on the parent(s), guardian(s) or custodian(s) of the student subject to and in noncompliance with the provisions of the Compulsory School Attendance Law.

(c) If unexcused absences continue after written notice of habitual truancy, as provided in Subsection B of this section has occurred, the student shall be reported to the Indian Child Welfare Act (ICWA) office of the Pueblo of Picuris for an investigation as to whether the student shall be considered to be a neglected child or a child in a family in need of services because of habitual truancy and thus subject to the provisions of the Pueblo of Picuris Children's Code [32A-1-1 NMSA 1978]. In addition to any other disposition, the Children's Court may order the habitual truant's driving privileges to be suspended for a specified time not to exceed ninety (90) days on the first finding of a habitual truancy and not to exceed one year for a subsequent finding of habitual truancy.

(d) If, after review by the Pueblo of Picuris ICWA Office, a determination and finding is made that the habitual truancy by the student may have been caused by the parent(s) of the student, then the matter will be referred by the Pueblo of Picuris IWCA Office to the Pueblo of Picuris Children's Court for filing of charges allowed under the Compulsory School Attendance Law. Charges against the parent may be filed in the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court.

(e) A parent of a student, after receiving written notice as provided in Subsection B of this section and after the matter has been reviewed in accordance with Subsection D of this section, knowingly allows the student to continue to violate the Compulsory School Attendance Law shall be guilty of a Class C Offense. Upon the first conviction, a fine of not less than twenty- five dollars ($25.00) or more than five hundred dollars ($500.00) may be imposed, or the parent of the student may be ordered to perform community service. If violations of the Compulsory School Attendance Law continue, upon the second and subsequent convictions, the parent(s) of the student who knowingly allows the student to continue to violate the Compulsory School Attendance Law shall be guilty of a Class B Offense and shall be subject to a fine of not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or imprisonment for a definite term not to exceed six (6) months or both.
