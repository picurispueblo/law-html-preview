Title 12 Medical Cannabis

Chapter 12.01 General Provisions

12.01.010 Short Title.

This title may be cited as the Medical Cannabis Code.

Prior Codifications

§12.01.010 was formerly codified as 12-1-1

12.01.020 Findings and Purpose.

The Pueblo of Picuris finds and declares that:

(a) The Pueblo of Picuris is a federally recognized Indian tribe and has sovereign powers of self- government to make and enforce their own laws, rules and regulations for medical cannabis activities within tribal lands.

(b) The Pueblo of Picuris has sovereign immunity from suit.

(c) The Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Council has decided to open specific tribal lands within its jurisdiction on to the cultivation, processing, use, possession, distribution and research of medical cannabis by enacting this title to the Pueblo of Picuris Law and Order Code.

(d) The purpose of this Medical Cannabis Code is to civilly regulate those persons, licensed businesses, and lands within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris with respect to medical cannabis.

(e) The Medical Cannabis Code allows for the beneficial use of medical cannabis, upon written recommendation of a medical provider, in a civilly regulated system for the purpose of alleviating symptoms caused by debilitating medical conditions.

(f) In 1997, New Mexico passed the Lynn and Erin Compassionate Use Act to enable persons residing in New Mexico to obtain and use medical cannabis legally under limited, specified circumstances. The Pueblo of Picuris will cooperate with the State of New Mexico on matters of mutual concern, including law enforcement and public safety.

(g) This Medical Cannabis Code regulates the lawful cultivation, processing, use, possession, distribution and research of medical cannabis in a manner consistent with federal law enforcement guidelines and priorities, and is not preempted by, nor does it violate any federal law.

(h) On October 28, 2014, the U.S. Department of Justice issued it Policy Statement on Cannabis Issues in Indian Country (Wilkinson Memo) which recognized the trend of states toward legalization of medical cannabis, and acknowledged that in exercising its national sovereignty Indian tribes may enact similar laws, consistent with the policy priorities therein. This title is in compliance with the Wilkinson Memo.

(i) The recorded use of cannabis goes back five thousand years. Modern medical research has confirmed the therapeutic value of cannabis in treating a wide array of debilitating medical conditions. Cannabis has many accepted medical uses in the United States, and around the world, having been recognized by public health organizations, including the Epilepsy Foundation, the American College of Physicians, the American Nurses Association, the National Institute of Health, and the National Institute of Drug Abuse.

(j) The Pueblo of Picuris joins the tribes, states and other jurisdictions in the passage of medical cannabis laws to improve and support health and wellness, ensure a lawful and well-regulated medical cannabis program, and to provide fiscal benefits to the Pueblo of Picuris and its tribal members.

12.01.030 Authority to Establish Medical Cannabis Program.

(a) This title hereby authorizes the Pueblo of Picuris to establish a Medical Cannabis Program to meet the medical needs of tribal members and qualified patients, and to ensure an adequate supply of medical cannabis for the number of patients in the registry, and to provide the right to use and possess cannabis for medical purposes when recommended by a medical provider who has determined that the person's health would benefit from the use of cannabis in the treatment of a debilitating medical condition.

(b) In order to further the goals and to provide an additional source of governmental revenue, the Tribe has adopted this title, which shall be liberally construed to fulfill the purposes for which it has been adopted.

(c) The Medical Cannabis Program shall have an Executive Committee for administrative decision-making as specified herein, and to promulgate Program Rules as necessary, for adoption by the Tribal Council.

(d) The Medical Cannabis Program shall hire a Program Manager to fulfill the administrative purposes and achieve program implementation who shall report to the Executive Committee for supervision.

(e) Nothing in this title shall be deemed to be in conflict with the Controlled Substances Act, 21 U.S.C. section 801 et seq. or to otherwise permit any activity that is lawfully prohibited under that Act.

(f) Nothing in this title shall be deemed to create a right for individuals to grow and distribute cannabis.

(g) Nothing in this title shall be construed to (1) allow persons to engage in conduct that endangers others or causes a public nuisance; (2) allow the diversion of medical cannabis to anyone other than the registered cardholder; (3) allow any activity relating to the cultivation, distribution, or consumption of cannabis that is otherwise illegal.

(h) The Medical Cannabis Program shall operate to (1) prevent the distribution of cannabis to persons under 21 years of age; (2) prevent the revenue from the sale of cannabis from going where it is legal to other states; (4) prevent cannabis activity that is legal under this title from being used as a cover or pretext for the trafficking of illegal drugs or other illegal activity; (5) prevent violence and the use of firearms in the cultivation and distribution of cannabis; (6) prevent drugged driving and the exacerabation of other adverse public health consequences associated with the use of cannabis; (7) prevent the growing of cannabis on public lands and the attendant public safety and environmental dangers posed by cannabis production on public lands; and (8) prevent the possession and use of cannabis on federal property.

12.01.040 Sovereign Immunity.

(a) The Pueblo of Picuris hereby declares that it is immune from suit under the doctrine of sovereign immunity. All tribal officials and employees, including members of the Tribal Council, and officers and directors of all federal and tribal corporations, acting within their scope of authority and within their scope of duty, are immune from suit for any acts of omissions, pursuant to this title.

(b) Nothing in this title shall be construed to limit or waive the sovereign immunity of the Tribe, Tribal Council, or the Board of Directors of the Three Eagles, Inc. for any purpose, expressly or impliedly.

(c) Tribal Corporations that are federally chartered, or chartered under tribal law, may negotiate limited waivers of sovereign immunity for purposes of doing business on tribal lands, pursuant to this title.

12.01.050 Jurisdiction.

(a) The Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court retains general jurisdiction over all matters arising from this Medical Cannabis Code.

(b) People who enter the tribal lands, or property, of the Pueblo of Picuris, shall be deemed to have consented to the civil jurisdiction of the Tribe. In additions, any person who utilizes this Medical Cannabis Code, in any manner, shall have consented to the civil jurisdiction of the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Court for any cause of action arising under this title.

12.01.060 Taxation of Medical Cannabis.

(a) The Pueblo of Picuris may impose a sales tax comparable to the State and local sales taxes on the sale of medical cannabis.

(b) The tribe may allow an exemption from tax for sales to the tribe, tribal enterprises, tribal-owned businesses, tribally chartered corporations, or tribal members, on medical cannabis cultivated within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris original land grant.

(c) Medical cannabis used in the course of medical treatments by a clinic, hospital, or similarly situated facility owned and operated by a federally recognized Indian tribe within its exterior boundaries may be exempted from taxation under the terms of this title and federal Indian law.

12.01.070 Full Faith & Credit; Comity.

(a) The Full Faith and Credit Clause of the United States Title (See 28 U.S.C. 1738) and the New Mexico Foreign Judgments Act (See NMSA 39-4A-3 1978) required the recognition and enforcement of Tribal Court Orders and Administrative Orders when doing so does not offend the public policy of the enforcing State.

(b) The Pueblo of Picuris Medical Cannabis Registry Identification Cards are consistent with the laws and public policy of the State of New Mexico, who's Department of Health administers the New Mexico Medical Cannabis Program pursuant to the Lynn and Erin Compassionate Use Act.

(c) Comity provides for a mechanism to be able to move formal orders across boundaries for uniformity and symmetry of policy between foreign nations and recognizes the rights of citizens and other persons under the laws of neighboring jurisdictions.

(d) The Pueblo of Picuris Medical Cannabis Program shall coexist with the neighboring State of New Mexico in a stable regulatory framework of policies, guidelines, procedural protections and public health reforms.

(e) Tribes have the right to govern themselves. The Pueblo of Picuris Medical Cannabis Registry Identification Cards shall be recognized under principles of Full Faith & Credit and comity throughout the State of New Mexico.

12.01.080 Definitions.

(a) "Administrative Order" means a formal decision of an administrative program of the Pueblo of Picuris, including the issuance of a medical cannabis registry identification card.

(b) "Adult Applicant" means a patient twenty0one years of age or older.

(c) "Allowable Supply of Medical Cannabis" means the amount of cannabis that a qualifying patient may presumptively purchase and possess for the qualifying patient's personal medical use, but at no time shall it be greater than six (6) ounces.

(d) "Cannabis" refers to the plant Cannabis, including constituents of the plant and the preparation of the plant.

(e) "Cannabis Business" means a licensed cultivation facility, processing facility, distribution facility, delivery companies, laboratories or any combination thereof.

(f) "Cannabis Concentrate" means specific subset of cannabis that was produced by extracting cannabinoids from cannabis. Types of cannabis concentrates include water-based cannabis concentrates, solvent-based cannabis concentrates, fat-based cannabis concentrates, and critical fluid cannabis concentrates.

(g) "Cannabis Product" means a product that contains cannabis or an extract thereof and is intended for use or consumption other than by inhalation, including but not limited to foodstuffs, extracts, oils tinctures and other similar products.

(h) "Cannabis Testing" means analyzing the safety and potency of cannabis.

(i) "Cardholder" means a qualifying patient, or nonresident cardholder, who has been issued and possesses a valid Pueblo of Picuris Medical Cannabis Patient Registry Identification Card pursuant to this title.

(j) "Critical fluid extraction cannabis concentrate" means a cannabis concentrate that was produced by extracting cannabinoids from cannabis the cannabis plant through the use of a solvent.

(k) "Cultivation" means the preparing and growing of the plant Cannabis.

(l) "Cultivation facility" means an entity licensed pursuant to this title that acquires, possesses, cultivates, delivers, transfers, transports, supplies, or sells cannabis and related supplies to qualifying cardholders.

(m) "Debilitating Medical Condition" means any condition for which treatment with medical cannabis would be beneficial, as determined by a patient's medical provider, or any other debilitating medical condition, or its symptoms, provided in this title.

(n) "Delivery Company" means a licensed cannabis business whose purpose is to deliver medical cannabis to qualified validated cardholders.

(o) "Distribution Facility" means any entity license pursuant to this title that acquires, possesses, delivers, transfers, transports, supplies, sells or dispenses cannabis or related supplies and education materials to qualifying cardholders.

(p) "Edible Cannabis Products" means products that contain or are infused with cannabis or an extract thereof; are intended for consumption by oral ingestion; and are presented in the form of foodstuffs, beverages, extracts, oils, tinctures, and other similar products.

(q) "Executive Committee" means the duly appointed Executive Committee of the Medical Cannabis Program, which is to be comprised of no less than three (3) persons who have knowledge of the regulation, cultivation and distribution of medical cannabis, and of tribal law and tribal economic development, to be chosen by the Three Eagles Board of Directors, to serve the best interest of the tribe and its members.

(r) "Fat-based cannabis concentrate" means a cannabis concentrate that was produced by extracting cannabinoids from cannabis through the use of butter, olive oil or other typical cooking fats as a solvent.

(s) "Foreign cultivation facility" means a licensed cultivation or licensed producer facility that is not under the jurisdiction of the Tribe.

(t) "Inventory control" means a process that may be used to monitor the chain of custody of cannabis from the point of cultivation to the consumer.

(u) "Management employees" means those employees who serve in a supervisor role of some nature.

(v) "Medical Advisory Board" means the board of advisors to the Medical Cannabis Program and to the Executive Committee comprised of three (3) medical providers.

(w) "Medical Cannabis" means useable cannabis, cannabis concentrates, and cannabis infused products used for a qualifying conditions pursuant to this title.

(x) "Medical Provider" means a person licensed in New Mexico to provide a patient with medical or health services, including professional medical providers such as nurses, nurse practitioners, doctors, psychologists, psychiatrists, and other licensed mental health medical providers, substance abuse counselors, licensed naturopaths, chiropractors, doctors of oriental medicine, and other medical providers approved by the Executive Committee, after consultation with the Medical Advisory Board. In order to recommend medical cannabis to a patient, the medical provider shall evaluate and conduct an assessment of the patient's medical history and medical records, if any and current medical condition, including an appropriate examination; in-person or via tele-medicine, and that the medical provider has consulted with the patient with respect to the patient's debilitating medical condition and the possible risks and benefits associated with medical cannabis.

(y) "Minor Applicant" means a patient less than twenty-one years of age.

(z) "Nonresident Medical Cannabis Card" means a currently valid Medical Cannabis Card, or its equivalent, issued by another tribe, state, district, territory, jurisdiction, or country that allows the person to use cannabis for medical purposes in the jurisdiction of issuance.

(aa) "Patient Registry" means the administrative database for valid medical cannabis patients established by the Pueblo of Picuris Medical Cannabis Program.

(bb) "Processing" means the harvesting of the plant Cannabis or the use of any process or equipment, including but not limited to dehydrators or humidifiers that may be necessary to convert raw cannabis plants or plant parts into a consumable product.

(cc) "Processing facility" means any entity licensed pursuant to this title at which cannabis is processed for distribution.

(dd) "Program Manager" means the person hired by the Executive Committee to manage and direct the Medical Cannabis Patient Registry and tribal administrative program.

(ee) "Pueblo of Picuris" means all lands within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo's original land grant; lands held by the United States of America in trust for the Pueblo of Picuris; and any lands which may in the future come within the jurisdiction of the Tribe by any lawful means.

(ff) "Qualifying Patient" means a person who has been diagnosed by a medical provider as having a debilitating medical condition pursuant to this title.

(gg) "Registry Identification Card" means a document issued by the Pueblo of Picuris that identifies a person as a registered qualifying medical cannabis cardholder. Cardholders and Nonresident Medical Cannabis cardholders may purchase and consume medical cannabis on certain designated premises and places on tribal land.

(hh) "Solvent-based cannabis concentrate" means a Cannabis Concentrate that was produced by extracting cannabinoids from cannabis through the use of glycerin, isopropyl alcohol, ethanol, or propylene glycol as a solvent.

(ii) "State" means the State of New Mexico.

(jj) "Tribe" means , and "Tribal" refers to the Pueblo of Picuris, a federally recognized Indian tribe.

(kk) "Tribal Court" means the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Courts as established pursuant to the Tribe's Law and Order Title.

(ll) "Water-based cannabis concentrate" means a Cannabis Concentrate that was produced by extracting cannabinoids from cannabis through the use of only water or ice.

(mm) "Written certification" means a document dated and signed by a Medical provider, stating that in the medical provider's professional opinion the patient is likely to receive therapeutic or palliative benefit from the medical use of cannabis to treat or alleviate the patient's debilitating medical conditions associated therewith.

Chapter 12.02 Patient Registry

12.02.010 Establishment of a Confidential Patient Registry.

(a) The Medical Cannabis Program shall create and maintain a confidential patient registry of patients who have applied for and are entitled to receive a registry identification card.

(b) All personal medical records and personal identifying information held by the patient registry shall be confidential information.

(c) No person shall be permitted to gain access to any information about patients in this registry, except for authorized employees of the Medical Cannabis Program, or employees of licensed cannabis businesses, in the course of their official duties.

(d) Authorized employees of local law enforcement agencies may be granted access to the information contained within the patient registry only for the purpose of verifying that an individual who has presented a registry identification card is lawfully in possession of such card.

12.02.040 Minors with Debilitating Medical Conditions.

(a) Minors who have a valid medical provider's written certification for a qualifying condition may consume medical cannabis under the supervision of their parent or guardian. The Medical Cannabis Program Manager may issue a registry identification care to a minor if:

(1) All application material are submitted and the minor's custodial parent or legal guardian provides proof of legal guardianship; and

(3) The minor's custodial parent or legal guardian consents to the use of medical cannabis by the minor; and

(4) The custodial parent or legal guardian agrees to control the dosage and frequency of the use of medical cannabis by the minor; and

(5) The custodial parent or legal guardian agrees not to divert any medical cannabis in their possession pursuant to this title.

12.02.110 Protection for the Use of Medical Cannabis.

(a) A medical cannabis cardholder who possesses a valid Pueblo of Picuris medical cannabis registry identification card is not subject to arrest, prosecution, or penalty in any manner, or denial of any right or privilege, including any civil penalty or disciplinary action by a court, or occupational or professional licensing board or bureau for the medical use of medical cannabis pursuant to his Title, provided the cardholder does not possess more than the allowable amount of medical cannabis, and that any medical cannabis being carried shall be enclosed in a sealed container, if it is being possessed or transported for personal medical use.

(b) There is a presumption that a qualifying cardholder is engaged in the medical use of cannabis pursuant to this title if the cardholder is in possession of a Pueblo of Picuris registry identification card and an amount of cannabis that does not exceed the allowable amount of cannabis.

(c) A medical provider shall not be subject to arrest, prosecution, or penalty in any manner, or denied any right or privilege, including, but limited to, civil penalty or disciplinary action by any other occupational or professional licensing board or bureau, solely for providing written certifications or for otherwise stating that, in the medical provider's professional opinion, a person is likely to receive therapeutic or palliative benefit from the medical use of cannabis to treat or alleviate the patient's debilitating medical condition, or symptoms associated with the debilitating medical condition, or symptoms associated with the debilitating medical condition.

(d) For the purposes of the laws of the Pueblo of Picuris, activities related to medical cannabis shall be considered lawful as long as they are in compliance with this title.

(e) No law enforcement officer employed by an agency which receives state, local, or federal government funds shall expend any resources, including the officer's time, to effect any arrest or seizure of medical cannabis, or conduct any investigations, on the sole basis of activity the officer believes to constitute a violation of the federal law if the officer has reason to believe that such activity is in compliance with this civil law Title.

(f) It is the public policy of the Pueblo of Picuris that lawful activities related to medical cannabis that are entered into by cardholders, medical cannabis businesses or licensed facilities, or medical cannabis business or licensed facility agents, are exempt from criminal penalties by this title.

12.02.170 Recognition of Nonresident Medical Cannabis Cards.

(a) As used in this section, "nonresident card" means a medical cannabis card issued by a State, Tribe, or jurisdiction other than Pueblo of Picuris; and is the functional equivalent of a registry identification card, as determined by the Program Manager, or a designee.

(b) The Pueblo of Picuris Medical Cannabis Program may verify and recognize a nonresident card under the following circumstances;

Chapter 12.03 Licensing

12.03.010 Cannabis Businesses; Licensure.

(a) The Executive Committee shall determine the appropriate number of licensed cannabis businesses, including cultivation facilities, processing facilities, distribution facilities, deliver companies, testing laboratories, or any combination thereof, based on the number of medical cannabis patients in the Medical Cannabis Program registry, and other relevant business considerations.

(b) Each licensed cannabis business under the jurisdiction of the Tribe shall be required to meet all licensing requirements under this title.

(c) The Executive Committee may assess a licensing fee per annum to each licensed cannabis business.

(d) All medical cannabis businesses must be licensed by the Executive Committee to lawfully operate within the exterior boundaries of the Pueblo of Picuris.

(e) All cultivation, processing, packaging and storing of cannabis must take place in a secure facility that may only be accessed by licensed persons and authorized visitors.

(f) All licensed facilities shall be constructed, maintained, and operated in a manner that adequately protects the environment and the health and safety of the public.

(g) The Executive Committee may limit the size, location, capacity, and hours of operation of all licensed facilities.

(h) No licensed facility may be located within 300 feet of a school, church, or daycare center.

(i) All licensed facilities shall adopt security and safety procedures and protocols, including lighting, signage, video surveillance, alarms, employee badges/wands, panic buttons, emergency response policies and operational policies that ensure the security of the crop and prevents diversion for non-medical purposes.

(j) Firearms are prohibited at all times at a licensed facility.

12.05.020 Cannabis Extraction and Concentrates.

(a) Processors are limited to certain methods, equipment, solvents, gases and mediums when creating cannabis extracts.

(b) Processors may use the hydrocarbons N-butane, isobutene, propane, or heptane or other solvents or gases exhibiting low to minimal potential human health-related toxicity approved by the Executive Committee. These solvents must be of at least ninety-nine percent purity and a processor must use them in a professional grade closed loop extraction system designed to recover the solvents, work in an environment with proper ventilation, controlling all sources of ignition where a flammable atmosphere is or may be present.

(c) Processors may use a professional grade closed loop CO2 gas extraction system where every vessel is rated to a minimum of six hundred pounds per square inch. The CO2 must be of at least ninety-nine percent purity.

(d) Processors closed loop systems, other equipment used, the extraction operation, and facilities must be approved for their use by the local fire code official and meet any required fire, safety, and building code requirements specified in the Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code.

(e) Other applicable standards including following all applicable fire, safety, and building codes in processing and the handling and storage of the solvent or gas.

(f) Processors may use heat, screens, presses, steam distillation, ice water, and other methods without employing solvents or gases to create kief, hashish, bubble hash, or infused dairy butter, or oils or fats derived from natural sources, and other extracts.

(g) Processors may use food grade glycerin, ethanol, and propylene glycol solvents to create extracts. All ethanol must be removed from the extract in a manner to recapture the solvent and ensure that it is not vented into the atmosphere.

(h) Processors creating cannabis extracts must develop standard operating procedures, good manufacturing practices, and a training plan prior to producing extracts for the marketplace. Any person using solvents or gases in a closed looped system to create cannabis extracts must be fully trained on how to use the system, have direct access to applicable material safety data sheets and handle and store the solvents and gases safely.

(i) A processing facility that engages in the production of cannabis concentrates, regardless of the method of extraction or type of concentrate being produced, must:

(1) Ensure that the space in which any Cannabis Concentrate is to be produced is a fully enclosed room and clearly designated;

(2) Establish a standard operating procedure for each method used to produce a cannabis concentrate;

(3) Establish written quality control procedures designed to minimize any potential risks to employees or contamination of cannabis products;

(4) Ensure that all equipment, counters, and surfaces used in the production of cannabis concentrate is thoroughly cleaned after the completion of each production of a cannabis concentrate;

(5) Provide adequate training prior to individual engaging the production of a cannabis concentrate;

(6) Maintain clear and comprehensive records that document every phase of each step in the production of the cannabis concentrate, which must include the name and license number of every employee that worked on the production of that cannabis concentrate; and

(7) Ensure that the room in which cannabis concentrate shall be produced contains an emergency eye-washing station.
