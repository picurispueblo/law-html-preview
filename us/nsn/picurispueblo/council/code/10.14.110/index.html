Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

10.14.110 Powers and Responsibilities of Guardian.

(a) Except as otherwise specifically ordered or limited by the Court:

(1) A guardian of a minor shall have the right to take or provide for the custody of the minor and shall be required to care for the health, safety, and welfare of such a minor and provide for their education and medical care as needed or appropriate.

(2) A general guardian or guardian of the estate of property of a minor shall have the authority to invest, manage and dispose of the property of the minor in a prudent and reasonable manner and expend such portions of the estate, income and then principle, as he/she shall deem reasonably necessary for the support, care, including medical care, and education of the minor given the size and nature of the estate and the station in life and needs of the minor.

(3) A guardian ad litem shall have the power and authority to represent a minor's best interests in actual, threatened or contemplated litigation or other proceedings of a legal nature (other than of a criminal nature and/or under this Code), and to employ counsel, and settle or compromise suits or claims, subject to the approval of the Court.

(4) A guardian of any kind may petition the Court for authority to do any act about which he/she is uncertain of his/her authority, and the Court may grant such authority, after such notice and hearing, if any, as the Court may direct, if such appears to be consistent with the best interests of the minor.

(5) A guardian of any kind shall stand in a fiduciary relationship to the minor; shall exercise a high degree of care in managing the estate of his/her ward; shall derive no personal benefit of any kind from his/her management of the estate of his/her ward; and shall be civilly liable to said ward for any losses to the estate attributable to a breach of these duties. Action to enforce such liability may be brought by the ward or a subsequently appointed guardian on behalf of the ward within two (2) years after the appointment of the new guardian or the removal of the incompetency or the arriving at the age of majority.
