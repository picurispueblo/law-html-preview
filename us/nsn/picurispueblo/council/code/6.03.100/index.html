Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

6.03.100 Petition and Response.

(a) Except as otherwise provided, in the dissolution of marriage action, the petition shall state the following:

(1) The name and date of birth of the parties, their social security numbers and Tribal Enrollment (census) numbers, occupation, date and place of marriage and facts relating to the residence of each respective party to include mailing and physical addresses of each party. A certified copy of marriage license must accompany the petition for dissolution of marriage.

(2) The name, date of birth, and enrollment number of each minor child of the parties; including certified copies of birth certificates, enrollment papers and Certificates of Indian Blood.

(3) A list of all property, individually as well as jointly owned and the location of said property; including all financial accounts, vehicles and real property.

(4) A statement that the marriage is irretrievably broken or that the parties agree to a dissolution of marriage.

(5) Whether or not an action for divorce or legal separation by either of the parties was or has at any time been filed or is pending in any court. Copies of such documents must be attached to the petition.

(6) If the parties have entered into any written agreement regarding support, custody, visitation, and/or division of property. Copies of all documents must be submitted to the Court.

(7) The relief requested of the Court.

(b) During the pendency of action, both parties are prohibited from and may be held in contempt of court for harassing, intimidating, physically abusing, and/or imposing any restraint on the other party or any minor child or dependent person.

(c) During the pendency of action, the parties are expressly prohibited from and may be held in contempt of court for encumbering, concealing, damaging, destroying, transferring or otherwise disposing of property owned by either of the parties. Property may be disposed of, during the pendency of action, by either party only with the permission of the Court.

(d) During the pendency of action, the parties are prohibited from and may be held in contempt of court for doing any of the following without the written consent of the other party or by an Order of this Court.

(1) Establishing a residence with the parties' minor child or children outside the State of New Mexico.

(2) Removing the parties' minor child or children from the Pueblo of Picuris and the State of New Mexico;

(e) Either of the parties to the marriage may initiate the action. The party who initiates the action or his/her attorney or advocate must sign the petition. Both parties and their respective attorney/advocate shall sign a joint petition if both parties are jointly filing an uncontested petition.

(f) The summons to the opposite party must be made by registered mail, return receipt requested or proper personal service. If only one party files the petition, the respondent will have twenty (20) days, from date of service, in which to file a written response with the Court.
