Pueblo of Picuris Tribal Law and Order Code

10.12.210 Voluntary Termination of Parental Rights.

(a) Initial Procedures: The Court may terminate parental rights when the parent desires to voluntarily give up such rights. In assuming jurisdiction of a voluntary termination of parental rights proceeding, the Court shall require that:

(1) No voluntary termination shall occur before a child is ten (10) days old;

(2) No voluntary termination shall occur until a written report is submitted to the Court by a Tribally recognized social services agency indicating that social services and counseling have been offered to the parent, that the consequences of the parent's actions have been fully explained and are understood by the parent, and that such action is in the best interest of the child; or

(3) A parent may waive in writing, before a Judge of the Court, his/her right to appear at a hearing or the right to notice of hearing, or both, and the Court shall assure that such waiver is knowing and voluntary.

(b) Counseling: In any proceeding for voluntary termination of parental rights, if the Court has reasonable doubt as to the emotional state of mind of the petitioner/parent or as to the petitioner's ability to understand the consequences of his/her decision, the Court shall place the child with extended family or foster care on the Pueblo of Picuris, for a period not to exceed thirty (30) days in order to allow the parent to consider his/her decisions. Further, the Court shall order legal and psychological counseling for the parent in order to assure his/her understanding of the consequences of his/her decision and a report of the results of such counseling shall be made to the Court.

(c) Procedures After Counseling: Immediately after the end of the thirty (30) day period, the Court shall, based on the report submitted:

(1) Return custody of the child to the parent;

(2) Process the petition for voluntary termination of parental rights; or

(3) Extend the period for no more than thirty (30) days to allow additional counseling. At the expiration of the additional counseling, and based upon the reports about the results of the counseling, the Court shall proceed as allowed by this Code.
